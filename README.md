## What Is This?

This repository contains the **fully open-sourced design, process documentation, and patent** for a homogeneous ion exchange membrane that can be fabricated using inexpensive, off-the-shelf precursors — no specialized suppliers or industrial equipment required.

Ion exchange membranes are a critical component in electrochemical systems used for:
- Precious and rare earth metal recovery
- Water purification and desalination
- Electroplating and electrorefining
- Fuel cells and flow batteries

Commercially available membranes (such as Nafion) can cost hundreds of dollars per square meter. **This open-source design dramatically lowers that barrier**, making advanced electrochemistry accessible to small labs, startups, researchers, and DIY builders worldwide.

This project is part of [Rowow LLC](https://rowow.net)'s mission to build a fully domestic, closed-loop electrochemical precious metal recovery industry — one that is eco-sustainable, competitive, and accessible.

## Why This Matters

The United States currently has very limited domestic capacity for critical minerals and platinum group metals. Most of the world's rare earth extraction and processing is concentrated in Asia and Africa. Our approach aims to change that by:

- 🏭 **Enabling local production** — all precursors are available domestically
- ♻️ **Reducing environmental impact** — closed-loop systems minimize chemical waste
- 💰 **Lowering the cost barrier** — from hundreds of dollars per sq/m to a few dollars
- 🔬 **Empowering the open-source community** — anyone can build, test, and improve this
