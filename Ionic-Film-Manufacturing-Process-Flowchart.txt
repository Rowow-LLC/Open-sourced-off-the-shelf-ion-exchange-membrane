================================================================================
OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This document outlines the step-by-step manufacturing process for fabricating a
low-cost, homogeneous ion exchange membrane (ionic film) from off-the-shelf
precursor materials. No specialized industrial equipment required.

The ionic film serves as the selective ion transport layer in Rowow LLC's
closed-loop electrochemical precious metal recovery system (SEM TECH).

================================================================================
MATERIALS REQUIRED
================================================================================

Primary Materials:
  - Ion exchange water softener resin (sulfonated polystyrene beads)
  - PVC cement (contains THF + PVC polymer in solvent)
  - Methylene blue dye (optional — for cation selectivity testing)

Equipment:
  - Mortar and pestle (or blender/grinder)
  - Flat glass surface or silicone mat
  - Spatula or palette knife
  - Safety: nitrile gloves, eye protection, ventilation

================================================================================
MANUFACTURING STEPS
================================================================================

PHASE 1 — RESIN PREPARATION
Step 1: Obtain granular cation exchange resin (water softener type)
Step 2: Dry resin — air-dry 24-48h or oven at 60C for 2-4h
Step 3: Pulverize to fine powder (target < 200 micron particle size)

PHASE 2 — BINDER MIXING
Step 4: Use PVC cement as-is (work in ventilated area — THF fumes)
Step 5: Mix resin powder + PVC cement at ~70% resin : 30% cement (by volume)
        until a thick, homogeneous paste forms
Step 6: Adjust consistency — too thick: add cement; too thin: add resin

PHASE 3 — FILM CASTING
Step 7: Place silicone mat or glass on level surface
Step 8: Spread paste to uniform 0.5–2mm thickness
Step 9: Allow THF solvent to evaporate — do not disturb for 30-60 min

PHASE 4 — CURING
Step 10: Cure 24h at room temperature (or 4-6h at 40-50C)
Step 11: Peel film, inspect for cracks/bubbles, trim edges
Step 12: Soak in DI water or 0.1M NaCl for 1-4h to hydrate ion groups

PHASE 5 — QUALITY TESTING
Step 13: Visual inspection — uniform, flexible, no large defects
Step 14: Conductivity test — measure ionic conductivity vs. control
Step 15: Selectivity test — methylene blue dye test (cation membrane blocks dye)

================================================================================
ANION EXCHANGE MEMBRANE VARIANT
================================================================================

Substitute anion exchange resin (quaternary ammonium type) for cation resin.
Same PVC cement binder and casting process applies.

================================================================================
TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

Cracks during drying   -> Increase PVC cement ratio; dry more slowly
Brittle film           -> Add more PVC cement; ensure full hydration
Poor conductivity      -> Increase resin content; reduce thickness; check hydration
Film sticks to surface -> Use release agent; allow full cure before peeling

================================================================================
SAFETY NOTES
================================================================================

- THF (in PVC cement) is highly flammable. Keep away from flames.
- Work in well-ventilated area or use respiratory protection.
- Wear nitrile gloves (THF penetrates latex).
- Dispose of THF waste per local regulations.
- Resin dust: avoid inhalation.
