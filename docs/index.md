# Open-Source Ion Exchange Membrane

A homogeneous cation or anion exchange membrane you can make for under $1 per square foot using off-the-shelf materials: water softener resin, PVC or CPVC, and common solvents. No sulfonation, no specialty suppliers, no industrial equipment. Dedicated to the public domain under CC0 1.0.

Developed by [Rowow LLC](https://rowow.net) as part of SEM TECH, an open-source electrochemical platform for critical mineral recovery, acid regeneration, and metal refining.

## Why It Matters

Commercial ion exchange membranes cost roughly $100 per sheet, which locks small labs, startups, and researchers out of membrane electrochemistry. This recipe drops that cost by about two orders of magnitude and works in harsh conditions: pH 0 solutions, high oxidizing potentials, months of exposure.

Applications include precious metal and rare earth recovery, redox flow batteries, electrodialysis, acid and base generation, water treatment, and electroplating.

## How It Works

Commercial ion exchange resin beads are already functionalized. The trick is mechanical: pulverize the beads into fine powder, disperse the powder in PVC or CPVC dissolved in a solvent such as THF, cast the mixture as a film, and dry. The result is a homogeneous membrane with resin functionality distributed through a chemically durable matrix that bonds directly to PVC cell bodies with ordinary pipe cement.
